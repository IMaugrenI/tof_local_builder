# Commercial and future license policy

## Current state

The repository currently contains publicly released AGPL-3.0 material.
That already-granted public license is not revoked for those releases.

## Future direction

The author may publish future versions under a stricter source-available or commercial license.
A likely direction is BUSL-style future licensing for later-distributed versions.

## Boundary rule

Any future license change applies only to material that the copyright holder is able to relicense.
It does not override:

- previously granted AGPL rights for earlier public versions
- third-party licenses
- upstream licenses
- dependency licenses
- trademark and naming restrictions already stated elsewhere

## Commercial use

Commercial or internal organizational use beyond the active public license grant may require a separate written license from the copyright holder.

## Practical reading

- earlier public versions keep their earlier license
- future versions may become stricter
- mixed-license situations must respect each applicable license separately
